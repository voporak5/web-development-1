Wireframing

Frankly this topic is not all that complicated, but in a nutshell wireframing is preparing a skeleton of the website structure to give stakeholders (whoever you are making the website for) or even yourself an idea of what the website will look like.

Up to this point you have all probably realized how time consuming building a website is but imagine you spent the same amount of time or more to then be told what you built was incorrect. It would be a huge waste of time and money to have to start over or worse, you are not compensated for the extra time you have to spend rebuilding it correctly.

Putting the design on paper (or digital) and getting approval on a design beforehand clears up any confusion there might be as well as saving time and money. Plus, when it is time to build the site everyone on the team has a reference they can refer to so fewer mistakes are made.

Keep in mind that the wireframe is an abstract design and it is not required to put in any actual text or images that may appear on the final site. You are simply allocating space for elements. Typically you might place a rectangle with an X going through it to represent an image and a bunch of horizontal lines to represent where bodies of text may go.

Tools

There are many tools you can use to put together a wireframe for a client in addition to good ol pen and paper. Some are free and some are not.

Check out This link for a list of some of the "best" tools.

Some of the tools simply draw out wireframes and some are interactive that you could potentially hand to a client and say this is what your website will look and feel like. It might feel like at that point you may as well use the tool to build the website but then you lose control of the backend. We refer to these kind of tools as WYSIWYG (wiz-e-wig) What You See Is What You Get.

The tool I use most (not specificaly for websites) is draw.io. It is simple and straight to the point. Plus it gives me an option of where to store my designs such as google drive or dropbox allowing me to load them later.
